What is Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (GHGs), mainly carbon dioxide (CO₂), that are emitted directly or indirectly by an individual, organization, event, or product. It is usually measured in tons of CO₂ equivalent (CO₂e) per year.

Why Calculate Your Carbon Footprint?

Every individual contributes to their nation's carbon footprint. National footprints are calculated by examining energy consumption, land use, and Industrial production, which generate emissions. On average, individuals contribute 2.4 tonnes to their nation's 6.5 tonnes footprint.
Calculating your personal carbon footprint helps identify your top emission sources. This awareness enables you to take targeted actions to reduce your impact, such as changing transportation habits or adopting energy-efficient practices.

What is CO2e?

CO2e, or carbon dioxide equivalent, is a standard unit for measuring carbon footprints. The idea is to express the impact of different greenhouse gases (GHG) in terms of the amount of CO, that would create the same amount of warming. That way, a carbon footprint consisting of lots of different greenhouse gases can be expressed as a simple single number.

What is a greenhouse gas?

A greenhouse gas is any gas in the atmosphere that traps heat. They let sunlight pass through the atmosphere, but they prevent the heat that the sunlight brings from leaving the atmosphere. The main GHGs in the Earth's atmosphere are water vapour, carbon dioxide(CO2), methane (CH/, nitrous oxide (N0), and ozone.

Why do greenhouse gases matter?

GHGs occur naturally in the Earth's atmosphere, and they are very important, otherwise our planet would be too cold and life as we know it would not exist. But human activities, such as the burning of fossil fuels,are increasing the levels of GHG's in the atmosphere, causing the climate to change. This is already negatively impacting the quality of life of millions of people and can affect the entire human family and nature if we continue to pollute these gases.
